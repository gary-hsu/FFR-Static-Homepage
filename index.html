Our Mission

Fawn's Family Rescue specializes in cat rescue, rehabilitation, community outreach, fostering, and rehoming of cats primarily from local Utah animal shelters and other rescue organizations. We focus on vulnerable cat populations that are often overlooked by the system, pulling approximately 10% of our cats with physical or behavioral special needs from at-risk shelters.

We operate Davis County's first Cat Lounge where we focus on ethical adoption practices, community growth, special needs cat care, and post-adoptive care programs to directly tackle the main causes of animal surrenders.

Who We Are

Founded by Areah Watterson and Gary Hsu in May 2022, Fawn's Family Rescue was established to continue life-saving operations as an official 501(c)(3) nonprofit. We are named after Fawn, a skittish chihuahua mix rescued from California who was at risk for euthanasia due to shelter capacity but thrived after months of rehabilitation.

After building relationships with rescues, shelters, and volunteers, we outgrew foster-based operations and opened our brick-and-mortar location as Davis County's first Cat Lounge. This free-roaming environment allows guests to interact, play with, and adopt our cats. Through community events like paint nights and partnerships with local businesses, our lounge has become a symbol that rescue work is a community effort.

Our Approach

Unlike traditional shelters, our cat lounge model provides a stress-free environment where cats can socialize while awaiting adoption. We focus on cats that are often first in line for euthanasia despite many successfully adjusting once given an environment where they can thrive with engagement, safe human interactions, and other cats from similar backgrounds.

We believe every cat deserves a chance regardless of age, medical history, or background. Our comprehensive post-adoptive care program ensures families have support for long-term success, directly addressing the housing and financial issues that cause surrenders.

Our Impact

Since October 2025, we have taken in and adopted out 550+ animals into new homes with an average intake of 200 animals annually. We see over 2,000 visitors to our lounge each year and our website receives over 25,000 visitors annually.

We partner with organizations to host affordable vaccine clinics administering hundreds of vaccines to reduce preventable diseases. We assist local shelter populations, coordinate efforts on animal hoarding cases, and help animals displaced by natural disasters.

Community Support

We rely on approximately 40 unique volunteers and fosters yearly, contributing around 2,400 hours of direct animal care, rehabilitation, advocacy, and operations. Our volunteers include experienced fosters providing special care, Trap-Neuter-Release volunteers supporting stray populations, and community service individuals who often transition into consistent volunteers.

As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, we depend on community generosity to continue this life-saving work. Every donation, volunteer hour, and adoption makes a direct impact on the cats in our care.
